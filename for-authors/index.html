Author Guidelines

Clear, school-appropriate, and publication-ready guidance

This page gives your contributors a structured overview of what to prepare before they submit a manuscript.

Who may submit

Tanglaw accepts manuscripts from junior high school students, senior high school students, teachers, and collaborative teams. Manuscripts can be written in English or in Filipino and should fit one of the journal sections stated in the Aims and Scope page.

Before submission

  • Ensure the manuscript is original and not under review elsewhere.
  • Confirm that all listed authors agree to the submission.
  • Prepare a clear title, abstract, keywords, and references.
  • Check that the manuscript fits the journal section and article type.

Recommended manuscript parts

  • Title
  • Author information
  • Abstract
  • Keywords
  • Introduction
  • Methods or procedure
  • Results and discussion
  • Conclusion or implications
  • References

Formatting guidance

Use the Tanglaw manuscript template and maintain a clean academic format with readable headings, consistent citations, and complete references. Tables and figures should be labeled clearly and cited appropriately in the text.

Authorship and ethics

Authors remain responsible for the accuracy, originality, and integrity of their work. Plagiarism, fabricated data, misleading authorship, and undisclosed conflicts are not acceptable. The journal may request revisions, clarification, or supporting documents where necessary.

AI-assisted writing disclosure

If AI tools are used for language support, editing assistance, or other limited functions, authors should disclose such use when required by the editorial office. Authors remain fully responsible for the manuscript content, claims, references, and interpretation.

After submission

  • The editorial office performs an initial screening.
  • Suitable manuscripts are sent for peer review.
  • Authors may be asked to revise the manuscript.
  • Accepted manuscripts are prepared for rolling online publication.
