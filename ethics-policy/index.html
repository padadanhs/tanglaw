Publication Integrity

Ethics policy for contributors and editors

Clear expectations help establish credibility and trust in a school-based research journal.

Ethics Policy

Tanglaw expects honesty, originality, and responsible scholarship from all contributors. Submitted manuscripts should not contain plagiarism, fabricated or falsified information, misleading authorship, or substantial uncredited reuse of existing work.

Author responsibilities

  • Submit only original work.
  • List only legitimate contributors as authors.
  • Disclose relevant conflicts of interest.
  • Report methods and findings accurately.
  • Cooperate with editorial queries and corrections.

Editorial responsibilities

  • Evaluate manuscripts fairly.
  • Protect confidential submissions.
  • Manage peer review responsibly.
  • Address ethical concerns when raised.
  • Issue corrections, clarifications, or removals when needed.

Misconduct and action

If ethical concerns arise, the editorial office may request clarification, supporting files, revision, withdrawal, correction, or other appropriate editorial action.
