Open Access

Free public access to school-based scholarship

The journal is designed to be widely accessible, easy to share, and friendly to school communities.

Open Access Policy

Tanglaw is intended as an open-access journal. Published articles should be freely available online so that students, teachers, schools, and community readers can access scholarly work without subscription barriers.

Access model

  • No paywall for readers
  • No article processing fee stated by the journal
  • Public web access through the journal website

Licensing note

Tanglaw states that articles may be licensed under CC BY 4.0 unless otherwise noted on the article page or in the published file.

Why open access matters

An open-access model is especially fitting for school-based research because it widens visibility, supports learning communities, and gives student and teacher work a more meaningful public presence.
